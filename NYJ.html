Born A Crime by Trevor Noah

Trevor Noah narrates his story through his upbringing in South Africa during
apartheid by his single mother.

Chapter 1: Run

Trevor Noah explains apartheid which exploits the tribal differences among the South
Africans, the Zulu who were the warrior people, and the Xhosa who termed
themselves as thinkers. The Zulu fought the colonial armies while the Xhosa tried
learning from them.

Trevor’s mother turns to Christianity where she figures that going to more churches
would get her more blessings because they faced challenges due to the racial
segregation caused by apartheid. Being raised in a Christian household, Trevor is
still not convinced that they should go to church every Sunday. Trevor’s mother is
governed by church and she wouldn't buy any of Trevor’s excuses to not go to
church. Trevor’s mother is determined to keep her son safe and she throws him out
of a moving car to save him from a gang attack. This is seen in African parents who do
anything just to protect their children.

Chapter 2: Born A Crime

Trevor witnessed violence among South Africans trying to figure out which group
would rule the country at a young age when Nelson Mandela was released from
prison. Trevor was born to a white father and a black mother, which was illegal at
that time. Apartheid had oppressive laws and this chapter shows the cruelty of
apartheid as Trevor’s existence was considered a crime. This meant that Trevor was
unable to attend school or play with other children of his own age, as he was termed
a threat to the apartheid government's racist ideology.

Connecting the issues with my county, Nairobi, there are land issues as Trevor
narrates. There is also inequality in churches where different
people attend different churches due to their socioeconomic status. This is a problem
that should be solved to ensure equality among all citizens.
