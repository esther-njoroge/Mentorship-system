Born A Crime by Trevor Noah

Trevor Noah narrates his story through his upbringing in South Africa during apartheid
by his single mother.

He explains apartheid which exploits the tribal differences among the South
Africans, the Zulu who were the warrior people, and the Xhosa who termed themselves
as thinkers. The Zulu fought the colonial armies while the Xhosa tried learning from
them.

Trevor’s mother turns to Christianity where she figures that going to more churches
would get her more blessings because they faced challenges due to the racial
segregation caused by apartheid. Each church offered different things. Being raised in
a Christian household, Trevor doesn't understand why they should go to church every
Sunday. Trevor’s mother is guided by church and she can't listen to any of Trevor’s excuses
to not go to church. She is determined to protect her son and keep him safe ; she throws him
out of a moving car to save him from a gang attack. This is seen in African parents who do anything just to protect their children.

Trevor witnessed violence among South Africans trying to figure out which group would
rule the country at a young age when Nelson Mandela was released from prison. He
was born to a white father and a black mother, which was illegal at that time. Apartheid had oppressive laws and this chapter shows the cruelty of apartheid as Trevor’s existence was considered a crime. This meant that Trevor was unable to attend school or play with other
children of his own age, as he was termed a threat to the apartheid government's racist
ideology.

Connecting the issues with my county, Nairobi, there are inequality issues as Trevor narrates.
This is seen in churches where different people attend different churches due to
their socioeconomic status. This is a problem that should be solved to ensure equality among
all citizens.
